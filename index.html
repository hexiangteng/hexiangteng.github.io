Short Bio

Dr. Xiangteng He is a Postdoctoral Researcher at the University of British Columbia, working with Leonid Sigal. Prior to joining UBC, he was a Research Assistant Professor at Peking University and conducted research at Alibaba DAMO Academy. He received his PhD from Peking University, where his doctoral research was recognized with the CCF Outstanding Doctoral Dissertation Award and the Baidu Scholarship.

Research

My research focuses on computer vision and multimodal learning, particularly representation learning, fine-grained multimodal understanding, and vision-language models, with applications in medical imaging and biodiversity monitoring. My recent works explore:

  • Predictive Representation Learning, Joint-Embedding Models, and World Models DSeq-JEPA
  • Fine-grained recognition and multimodal adaptation CVPR'26TIP'24IJCAI'24ACM MM'23
  • Vision-language understanding and cross-modal alignment ICLR'26AAAI'25AAAI'24ICCV'23
  • Generative multimodal modeling, evaluation, and layout generation ICCV'25CVPR'23ACM MM'23
  • Multimodal learning for medical and scientific data CVPR'26SCALE-VLP
